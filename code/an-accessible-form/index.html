Tips:

  • Placeholder text isn't a label. It should give an example of what input you should provide.
  • You need an actual visible label that is tied to the field it is labelling.
  • Indicate your required fields, both visually and with HTML attributes.
  • Make sure your tab order flows properly.
  • Make sure you have good focus indication.
  • Hitting 'Enter' should submit your form.
  • Group related fields (like radio buttons and checkboxes) together with fieldsets and legends
  • Use ARIA roles and attributes if you are doing anything beyond what semantic HTML supports. This form uses the following ARIA roles and attributes:
    • aria-describedby on the radio inputs.
    • aria-invalid and aria-errormessage on the invalid inputs.
    • role="alert" on the validation errors.

Things I didn't include:

  • Grouping of errors at the top of the form, with links to focus on the exact inputs.
  • Validation updating on blur or change; you have to resubmit the form to see a change in the errors.
